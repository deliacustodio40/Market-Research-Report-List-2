<p><p>General-purpose op amps are electronic devices widely used in various applications. There are two common types in the market: bipolar and CMOS. Bipolar op amps are known for their high output and input impedance, making them suitable for precise signals. They operate with both positive and negative voltage supplies, resulting in a wider dynamic range. On the other hand, CMOS op amps offer advantages such as low power consumption and compatibility with digital circuitry. They are commonly used in battery-operated devices or applications where low power consumption is crucial.</p></p>
